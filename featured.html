New Artists and Tracks

PinkPantheress released her debut studio album, "Heaven Knows," on November 10, 2023, following her 2021 debut mixtape, "To Hell With It." The album's journey was marked by delays, as PinkPantheress grappled with the pressure of meeting expectations. In interviews in 2022 and 2023, she expressed concerns and a desire to explore diverse musical styles before the album's eventual release.

The album's rollout included the lead single, "Boy's a Liar Pt. 2" featuring Ice Spice, released on February 3, 2023. Despite earlier speculations about an October release, the album's cover art, title, and tracklist were unveiled on October 12, coinciding with the release of "Capable of Love," the third single. With twelve tracks and featuring artists like Central Cee, Rema, and Kelela, "Heaven Knows" emerged as a significant milestone in PinkPantheress' musical evolution, showcasing her versatility and depth as an artist.

The first song on For All The Dogs Scary Hour Edition, “Red Button,” sees Drake delve into a darker mindset than previous releases. The track sets the tone for the following five songs, all of which express dark beats and lyrics.

The title, “Red Button,” alludes to the illuminated button on studio recording equipment, indicating an active recording is in process or a broadcast is underway.

This track delivers lyrical prowess combined with captivating rhythms, creating a musical experience that pushes boundaries and ignites emotions.

Embark on a sonic voyage with Andre 3000's groundbreaking album, "New Blue Sun." Experience a blend of innovation and artistry that redefines musical boundaries, offering a celestial experience for the ears and soul.

A departure album in the classic sense, New Blue Sun also feels like André has arrived. Its making came about organically, once he relocated from New York to LA. Instead of the OutKast origin story that started at Headland and Delowe, where Big Boi and Dre met their future Dungeon Family producers Organized Noize back in the day, this remix began with an unassuming trip to Erewhon — the chic LA health food chain where André bumped into percussionist and experimental jazz heavy, Carlos Niño.
